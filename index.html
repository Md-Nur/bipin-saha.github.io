Research

Enhancing Vehicle Detection in Adverse Weather with an Ensemble Approach and Explainable AI
This study addresses the challenge of vehicle detection under adverse weather conditions by proposing an ensemble approach that combines YOLOv11 and YOLOv12 models, resulting in improved detection performance with a mAP@0.5 increase from 0.631 to 0.6479 on the DAWN dataset. To ensure transparency and reliability, EigenCAM-based Explainable AI (XAI) techniques are employed to interpret and validate model predictions. The findings highlight the benefits of ensemble learning for robust detection and the crucial role of XAI in assessing model trustworthiness in challenging environments.

BNVD: A Comprehensive Dataset Advancing Vehicle Detection in Challenging and Underrepresented Regions
This research introduces BNVD, a vehicle detection dataset for Bangladesh, addressing the lack of region-specific data in autonomous navigation. BNVD includes 17 native vehicle classes, 17,326 images, and 81,542 annotated instances, covering diverse conditions like lighting, weather, and perspectives. Evaluations using YOLO models (v5–v8) achieve 0.848 mAP₀.₅ and 0.643 mAP (IoU 0.5–0.95), validating its effectiveness while highlighting challenges in real-world vehicle detection.

FORS-EMG: A Novel sEMG Dataset for Hand Gesture Recognition Across Multiple Forearm Orientations
This study introduces a novel multichannel sEMG dataset designed to account for dynamic factors like forearm orientation, which are often overlooked in existing datasets. Data were collected from 19 able-bodied participants performing 12 hand gestures across three forearm positions—supination, rest, and pronation—using eight EMG electrodes. The signal quality was validated using SNR and SMR metrics. The dataset was analyzed using both traditional machine learning classifiers (LDA, SVM, KNN) with five feature extraction methods and deep learning models (1D CNN, RNN, LSTM, and hybrids). The best performance was achieved by the LDA classifier with the SNTDF feature, reaching an F1 score of 88.58%. The publicly available dataset serves as a valuable resource for gesture recognition and prosthetic control research.

Waiting Time Prediction in Queue Management: Leveraging Machine Learning Approach
This study presents an intelligent and scalable queue management system (QMS) that leverages regression algorithms and an LSTM model to predict client waiting times in real time. The Voting Regressor achieved the best performance, with an R² score of 0.944 and an MAE of 1.828 minutes. The system dynamically allocates customers to service counters, optimizing resource use and enhancing efficiency. By addressing the shortcomings of traditional physical QMS—such as limited scalability and space/time inefficiencies—this solution offers a modern, cost-effective alternative for service environments.

Prediction of Waiting Time in Queues: An Ensemble Learning Approach
This study presents a robust AI-based approach to accurately predict waiting times in queues using ensemble tree-based regression models, including Random Forest, Extra Trees, Gradient Boosting, Histogram-Based Gradient Boosting, and a Voting Regressor. Among these, the Extra Trees Regressor achieved the best performance. The study also explored dimensionality reduction using PCA, which proved less effective than using the full feature set. Additionally, it addressed data imbalance in classification tasks using SMOTE, leading to improved accuracy, particularly for minority classes.

Numerical Modeling of CuSbSe2-based Dual-Heterojunction Thin Film Solar Cell with CGS Back Surface Layer
This study proposes a dual-heterojunction n-ZnSe/p-CuSbSe₂/p⁺-CGS solar cell, utilizing copper gallium selenide (CGS) as a back surface field (BSF) layer to mitigate interface recombination and Schottky barrier issues in conventional CuSbSe₂ cells. The BSF layer enhances efficiency through a tail-states-assisted upconversion technique, enabling better absorption of sub-bandgap light. Numerical simulations in SCAPS-1D optimize absorber thickness, doping, and defect densities, achieving a PCE of 43.77%, significantly outperforming the 27.74% PCE of single heterojunction designs, approaching the Shockley–Queisser efficiency limit.

An Efficient Approach for Appearance Based Eye Gaze Estimation with 13 Directional Points
The technical objectives are to develop a real-time eye gaze tracking system using a standard web or smartphone camera in unconstrained environments, implement an active appearance model for precise gaze estimation by isolating the sclera pixel area, and evaluate system performance using decision tree, random forest, and extra tree classifiers to achieve approximately 98% accuracy over 13 targets.
